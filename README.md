# Supplementary Reflection — *Judgment Cannot Be Delegated*

A supplementary essayistic note accompanying the second revision (R2) of a
manuscript currently under review at *Philosophy & Technology*
(manuscript ID **PHTE-D-26-00254**).

The document is offered to the reviewers as **optional reading**. It is
not part of the manuscript, and the assessment of the revision does not
depend on it. Its purpose is to share a reflection that the reviewers'
engagement has prompted and that exceeds the format of a standard
response letter.

## Document

- [`reflection_for_reviewers.pdf`](reflection_for_reviewers.pdf) — six pages, A4.

## Structure

The reflection is organised in five sections:

- **§A — Postulating, not inheriting.** The methodological reframing of the
  axiomatic system as postulation rather than inheritance from the
  philosophical tradition.

- **§B — The productive inversion.** How the framework re-grounds classical
  distinctions rather than defending them, and the kinds of circularity this
  allows it to avoid.

- **§C — A dialogue with the tradition — and with the present.** A
  confrontation of the framework with three classical debates (Aristotelian
  *phronēsis*, the Kantian humanity-as-end formula, and the Levinas–Arendt
  axis of responsibility and natality) and with two contemporary positions
  (Floridi on artificial agency; the Meaningful Human Control literature).

- **§D — Axiom 1 in two times.** The rewriting of the article's most delicate
  axiom as a minimal regulative postulate at the start, enriched only
  retrospectively in the conclusion.

- **§E — Two frontiers, not one.** The structural distinction between an
  empirical frontier (mobile with scale) and a categorical frontier (immobile
  by structure) in the interpolation/extrapolation argument.
