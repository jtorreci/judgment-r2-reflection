# Supplementary Materials — *Judgment Cannot Be Delegated*

Materials accompanying the second revision (R2) of a manuscript currently
under review at *Philosophy & Technology* (manuscript ID **PHTE-D-26-00254**).

## Documents

- [`reflection_for_reviewers.pdf`](reflection_for_reviewers.pdf) — six pages,
  A4. A supplementary essayistic note offered as **optional reading**. It is
  not part of the manuscript, and the assessment of the revision does not
  depend on it. Its purpose is to share a reflection that the reviewers'
  engagement has prompted and that exceeds the format of a standard response
  letter.

- [`judgment_diff.pdf`](judgment_diff.pdf) — fifty-five pages, A4. A rendered
  tracked-changes view documenting the differences between the round-1 and
  round-2 versions of the manuscript. Provided here for ease of inspection,
  alongside the `.tex` source uploaded through the editorial system.

## Structure of the reflection

The supplementary reflection is organised in five sections:

- **§A — Postulating, not inheriting.** The methodological reframing of the
  axiomatic system as postulation rather than inheritance from the
  philosophical tradition.

- **§B — The productive inversion.** How the framework re-grounds classical
  distinctions rather than defending them, and the kinds of circularity this
  allows it to avoid.

- **§C — A dialogue with the tradition — and with the present.** A
  confrontation of the framework with three classical debates (Aristotelian
  *phronēsis*, the Kantian humanity-as-end formula, and the Levinas–Arendt
  axis of responsibility and natality) and with two contemporary positions
  (Floridi on artificial agency; the Meaningful Human Control literature).

- **§D — Axiom 1 in two times.** The rewriting of the article's most delicate
  axiom as a minimal regulative postulate at the start, enriched only
  retrospectively in the conclusion.

- **§E — Two frontiers, not one.** The structural distinction between an
  empirical frontier (mobile with scale) and a categorical frontier (immobile
  by structure) in the interpolation/extrapolation argument.

## Access during peer review

For the duration of peer review, these documents are being shared with the
reviewers through an anonymous proxy that hides the author's identity. The
PDFs themselves contain no identifying metadata. The repository here exists
to host the files; the reviewers access them via the proxy URL provided in
the response letter, not via this page.

## After the editorial decision

If the article is accepted, the materials will be re-released under the
author's name. Otherwise they will remain available here for reference.
